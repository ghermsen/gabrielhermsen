Logistic Dashboard with Power BI

  • Project's Files

This Dashboard was created with some KPIs to analyze the performance of a company in the logistics industry. The first page of the Dashboard presents an analysis of the company's performance. The analysis is based on Revenue, Total Cost, Gross Income, Gross Income Margin and Average Ticket KPIs. On the second page of the Dashboard, an analysis of deliveries was performed, and KPIs such as OTIF, On Time, In Full and Orcer Cycle Time were analyzed.

Below there is a list of all measurements created using DAX so that your future projects can be replicated.

2 - Performance Page

(back to dashboard) | (list of measures)


2.1 - Revenue

(back to dashboard) | (list of measures)

The company's revenue is all the money received from its activities. It means that the revenue is everything that the company earns from the sale of services, products, financial investments, etc., in this project, the sum of everything in the shipping cost column.


Revenue = SUM(fRequest[Shipping Cost])

2.2 - Total Cost

(back to dashboard) | (list of measures)

Total Cost is the sum of all fixed or variable costs that a company has, in this project, the sum of everything in the total cost column.


Total Cost = SUM(fCost[TotalCost])

2.3 - Gross Profit

(back to dashboard) | (list of measures)

A company’s gross profit, is the revenue from all sources minus the firm’s cost of goods sold (represented by the measure Total Cost).


Gross Profit = [Revenue] - [Total Cost]

2.4 - Gross Profit Margin

(back to dashboard) | (list of measures)

Gross profit margin is a percentage value obtained from the gross profit and total revenue (the formula is: gross profit margin = gross profit / revenue).


Gross Profit Margin = DIVIDE([Gross Profit], [Revenue])

2.5 - Gross Profit Margin Last Year

(back to dashboard) | (list of measures)

This measure is used to compare with the actual gross profit margin and then sees the evolution over time.


Gross Profit Margin LY = 
CALCULATE(
    [Gross Profit Margin],
    SAMEPERIODLASTYEAR(dCalendar[Date])
)

2.6 - Number of Orders

(back to dashboard) | (list of measures)

This measure returns the total number of orders that the company obtained in the period.


Nr Orders = DISTINCTCOUNT(fRequest[Nrequest])

2.7 - Average Ticket

(back to dashboard) | (list of measures)

The average ticket represents the average amount spent on orders: the higher the amount, the more customers spend on orders.


Average Ticket = DIVIDE([Revenue], [Nr Orders])

3 - Shipment Page

(back to dashboard) | (list of measures)


3.1 - On-Time

(back to dashboard) | (list of measures)

The on-time delivery metric is used to assess their ability to fulfill a customer order by the promised delivery date.

First is calculated its absolute value:


# OnTime = 
CALCULATE(
    [Nr Orders],
    fRequest[OnTime] = "On Time"
)

Then, its percentage:


% OnTime = DIVIDE([# OnTime], [Nr Orders])

3.2 - In-Full

(back to dashboard) | (list of measures)

The in full returns all the orders that the customer gets strictly the amount they have ordered. In this project, it returns the orders that had no incidents.


% In Full = 
DIVIDE(
    CALCULATE(
        [Nr Orders],
        fRequest[Incidence] = 0),
    [Nr Orders]
)

3.3 - OTIF

(back to dashboard) | (list of measures)

The OTIF verifies that orders were delivered on time, without returns, and investigates whether the product has arrived within the specifications provided to customers at the time of purchase.


% OTIF = [% OnTime] * [% In Full]

3.4 - Order Cycle Time (OCT)

(back to dashboard) | (list of measures)

The order cycle time (OCT) measures the average time required to fulfill customer orders


OCT = 
AVERAGEX(
    fRequest, 
    DATEDIFF(
        fRequest[RequestDate],
        fRequest[DeliveryDate],
        DAY)
)
